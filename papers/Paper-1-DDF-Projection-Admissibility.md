

# **Paper 1 — The Dual Domain Framework: Projection, Admissibility, and the Emergence of Physical Structure**

## **Abstract**

We present the foundational structure of the Dual Domain Framework (DDF), in which the observable universe arises as a projection of an underlying infinite generative domain. We show that finite observability imposes strict constraints on this projection, leading to spectral selection, particle discreteness, and the emergence of physical constants as structural parameters. We further demonstrate that admissibility and propagation requirements force a hyperbolic operator structure, from which the propagation cone, Lorentz symmetry, and Clifford algebra arise. This establishes a unified structural origin for key features of physical theory without assuming spacetime, particles, or quantum structure a priori.
