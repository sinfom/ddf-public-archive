# Paper 2 — Operator Emergence and Propagation Structure in the Dual Domain Framework (DDF)

## Abstract

This paper develops the central operator structure governing the observable universe within the Dual Domain Framework (DDF). Building on Paper 1, where admissibility arises from projection constraints, we formalise how admissibility enforces propagation structure, leading to a hyperbolic operator with a well-defined characteristic cone. From this, we derive the emergence of a finite propagation speed \( c \), not as an empirical constant but as a structural constraint required for stability. We then show how first-order factorisation of the operator leads naturally to Dirac-type structure and the emergence of spinorial degrees of freedom. Physical constants are interpreted not as derived quantities but as stabilising parameters required for consistency of the projection.

---

## 1. Introduction

Paper 1 established the core ontology of DDF:

- A generative domain \( \Omega \)
- A projection operator \( P: \Omega \to U \)
- An admissibility condition selecting stable configurations in \( U \)

This paper addresses the next critical question:

> What mathematical structure must admissible configurations obey?

We show that admissibility enforces propagation constraints, which in turn require the existence of a governing operator \( L \). This operator is not assumed but emerges as the only structure consistent with stable projection.

---

## 2. Admissibility Implies Propagation Constraints

### 2.1 Stability Requirement

Admissible configurations must satisfy:

- Finite energy behaviour
- Structural coherence under perturbation
- No instantaneous global collapse

This excludes arbitrary configurations and imposes **locality of influence**.

### 2.2 Domain of Dependence

For any admissible configuration \( u(x,t) \), perturbations must propagate within a bounded region:

\[
\text{supp}(u(t)) \subseteq \text{light-cone region}
\]

This implies:

- No instantaneous propagation
- A finite maximal propagation speed

### 2.3 Emergence of Propagation Cone

From the above, admissible states must satisfy:

\[
|\omega| \leq c |k|
\]

This is not assumed but required for stability of projection.

## 3. Emergence of the Governing Operator

### 3.1 Necessity of Operator Structure

To enforce propagation constraints globally, the system must satisfy an operator equation:

\[
L u = 0
\]

Where \( L \) satisfies:

- Locality
- Finite propagation speed
- Stability under perturbation

### 3.2 Hyperbolic Structure

The propagation constraint implies that \( L \) must be **hyperbolic**, with principal symbol:

\[
p(\omega, k) = \omega^2 - c^2 |k|^2
\]

This defines the characteristic cone:

\[
p(\omega, k) = 0
\]

### 3.3 Interpretation of \( c \)

\[
c = \sup \frac{|\omega|}{|k|}
\]

Crucially:

- \( c \) is not derived from projection
- It is a **constraint parameter** required to stabilise the admissibility condition

## 4. From Second-Order Operator to First-Order Structure

### 4.1 Factorisation Requirement

Second-order operators admit factorisation:

\[
L = D^2
\]

Where \( D \) is a first-order operator.

### 4.2 Dirac-Type Operator

The minimal consistent factorisation leads to:

\[
D = \gamma^\mu \partial_\mu
\]

With:

\[
D^2 = \Box
\]

This introduces:

- Clifford algebra structure
- Spinorial degrees of freedom

### 4.3 Emergence of Spin

Spin arises as a structural requirement of factorisation:

- Not an added quantum property
- Not imposed externally
- But required to maintain consistency of the operator structure
