# Wave Group Velocity

## Purpose

This note derives the concept of **group velocity** from the dispersion relation implied by propagation rigidity.

It establishes how **signals and energy propagate** within the admissible propagation cone.

---

## Starting Point: Dispersion Relation

From propagation rigidity we obtain the linear dispersion relation

\[
\omega^2 = c^2 k^2
\]

which implies

\[
\omega = \pm c k
\]

where

- \( \omega \) is angular frequency
- \( k \) is the wave number
- \( c \) is the propagation constant determined by the cone slope.

---

## Phase Velocity

The **phase velocity** of a monochromatic wave is

\[
v_p = \frac{\omega}{k}
\]

For the linear dispersion relation this gives

\[
v_p = c
\]

Thus individual wave phases propagate along the boundary of the propagation cone.

---

## Group Velocity

Physical signals are not single-frequency waves but **superpositions of nearby modes**.

The propagation speed of a wave packet envelope is the **group velocity**

\[
v_g = \frac{d\omega}{dk}
\]

For the linear dispersion relation

\[
\omega = ck
\]

we obtain

\[
v_g = c
\]

---

## Interpretation

In a linear dispersion system:

- phase velocity equals group velocity
- signal propagation occurs at the cone slope \(c\)

Thus the propagation cone defines both

- allowable spectral structure
- the maximal speed of information propagation.

---

## Connection to the Wave Equation

The dispersion relation corresponds to the wave operator

\[
\Box = \partial_t^2 - c^2 \nabla^2
\]

which governs admissible propagation in the projected universe.
