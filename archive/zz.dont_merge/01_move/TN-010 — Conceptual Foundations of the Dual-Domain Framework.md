# Title : TN-010 — Conceptual Foundations of the Dual-Domain Framework

Status Conceptual framework only. No proofs.

Purpose Introduce the Dual-Domain Framework and its core idea: observable physics emerges through projection from a deeper domain.

Summary

The Dual-Domain Framework (DDF) proposes that the observable universe (the U-domain) is not fundamental but arises as a projection of a deeper infinite-dimensional structure termed the Generative Domain (G-domain).

The G-domain is characterised by:

- infinite-dimensional configuration space

- absence of intrinsic spacetime structure

- unrestricted harmonic structure

- nonlinear wave dynamics

The U-domain emerges through the action of a projection operator:

𝑃 : Ω  → 𝑈

where Ω denotes the configuration space of the G-domain. The projection imposes structural constraints that generate the effective properties observed in physical reality:

finite dimensionality of spacetime

- finite propagation speed

- gauge structure

- quantum discreteness

- gravitational geometry

Under this view, many elements of modern physics traditionally taken as fundamental—spacetime, locality, and relativistic invariance—are instead effective properties induced by projection.
Role in the framework
This note defines the conceptual architecture only. It must never be used as a premise for mathematical proofs.
