# Fermionic and Bosonic Sectors

Projected configurations may possess distinct topological structures. [file:1] Let a phase parameter \( \theta \) describe harmonic winding. [file:1] Phase topology determines the associated particle statistics. [file:2]

---

## Fermionic Modes

\[
\Psi_G(\theta + 2\pi) = -\Psi_G(\theta)
\] [file:1]

This condition defines **fermionic** modes and produces antisymmetric exchange behaviour. [file:1][file:2]

---

## Bosonic Modes

\[
\Psi_G(\theta + 2\pi) = \Psi_G(\theta)
\] [file:1]

This condition defines **bosonic** modes and produces symmetric exchange behaviour. [file:1][file:2]

---

## Interpretation

Fermion and boson statistics arise from topological phase structure of admissible projection modes, rather than from externally imposed quantum statistics. [file:1][file:2]
