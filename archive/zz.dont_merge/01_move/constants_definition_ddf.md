
# Definition: Physical Constants in the Dual‑Domain Framework (DDF)

## Purpose
Provide a clear conceptual definition of how physical constants are interpreted within the DDF research programme.

## Definition

In the Dual‑Domain Framework, physical constants are **structural parameters of the projection** from the generative domain Ω to the observable universe U.

They are not arbitrary numbers inserted into equations. 
Instead they are conversion scales linking different geometric structures that arise during the projection.

## Structural Role of Constants

Each constant corresponds to a relationship between two structural layers of the projected universe.

Examples:

Propagation geometry → c  
Phase‑space geometry → ℏ  
Curvature response → G

Thus constants act as **conversion scales between different geometrical descriptions of reality**.

## Interpretation

The constants are therefore:

- structural parameters of the projected universe
- conversion scales between physical quantities
- emergent from the mathematical relationships that define the projection

They function as the **building blocks that connect the different geometrical layers of the universe**.

## Implication

Changing one of these constants alters the relationships between the structural layers, which would fundamentally change the physical universe produced by the projection.
