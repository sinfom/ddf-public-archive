## Purpose

Record the current progress of the Dual Domain Framework (DDF) in explaining the origin of fundamental physical constants from projection structure. Summarise which constants have been structurally derived, by what mechanisms, and what remains to derive the fine‑structure constant \(\alpha\).

## Definitions

- Ω — generative domain.  
- U — observable universe.  
- \(P : \Omega \to U\) — projection from generative to observable domain.  
- Dirac operator \(D\) — central operator controlling propagation, quantisation, curvature, and gauge structure.  
- \(c\) — speed of light.  
- \(\hbar\) — Planck constant.  
- \(G\) — Newton’s gravitational constant.  
- \(\alpha\) — fine‑structure constant.  
- \(\Lambda\) — spectral cutoff scale associated with projection.

(Conceptual roles of these constants are detailed in F5 — Structural Role of Physical Constants in DDF.)

## Core Statement / Theorem

Within the current state of the DDF research programme:

- \(c\) (speed of light) is derived from **propagation rigidity** as a universal propagation bound.  
- \(\hbar\) (Planck constant) is derived from **phase‑space quantisation constraints** as a minimal phase‑space unit.  
- \(G\) (Newton’s constant) is derived **structurally** from the **spectral action of the Dirac operator**, with its value controlled by a projection‑induced spectral cutoff \(\Lambda\).  
- \(\alpha\) (fine‑structure constant) has not yet been derived; its value depends on a still‑missing determination of the particle spectrum and charge assignments produced by projection.

## Derivation / Explanation

### Core Idea of the Framework

The observable universe arises from a projection

\[
\Omega \rightarrow U
\]

The structure of this projection determines both physical laws and the constants that appear in the observable universe. The Dirac operator generated by the projection becomes the key object controlling: propagation, quantisation, curvature, and gauge structure.

### Constants Derived So Far

#### 1. Speed of Light \(c\)

- Origin: **Propagation rigidity**.  
- Mechanism: The projection imposes a bound on how information propagates, producing a universal propagation limit
  \[
  \text{propagation bound} \rightarrow c.
  \]
- Interpretation: \(c\) is the maximum transport speed allowed by the projection geometry.  
- Status: **Derived**.

#### 2. Planck Constant \(\hbar\)

- Origin: **Phase‑space quantisation constraints**.  
- Mechanism: Projection limits how precisely position and momentum can be resolved, producing a minimum phase‑space unit
  \[
  \text{phase‑space resolution} \rightarrow \hbar.
  \]
- Interpretation: \(\hbar\) measures the smallest meaningful phase‑space cell allowed by the projection.  
- Status: **Derived**.

#### 3. Newton’s Gravitational Constant \(G\)

- Origin: **Spectral action of the Dirac operator**.  
- Mechanism (schematic chain):
  \[
  \text{Dirac operator}
  \;\rightarrow\; \text{curvature in } D^2
  \;\rightarrow\; \text{heat‑kernel expansion}
  \;\rightarrow\; \text{Einstein–Hilbert action}
  \;\rightarrow\; \text{gravitational coupling}.
  \]
- Relation:
  \[
  G \sim \frac{\hbar c}{\Lambda^2},
  \]
  where \(\Lambda\) is a spectral cutoff imposed by the projection.
- Interpretation: \(G\) measures how strongly curvature responds to energy, with its magnitude set by \(\Lambda\).  
- Status: **Derived structurally** (exact numerical value depends on \(\Lambda\)).

### Spectral Cutoff from Projection

Projection restricts admissible states in phase space, implying a bounded Dirac spectrum

\[
|\lambda| \le \Lambda_{\text{proj}},
\]

so that

\[
\Lambda = \Lambda_{\text{proj}}.
\]

This spectral cutoff then determines the gravitational coupling. The framework has **derived the existence** of this cutoff structurally; the **exact numerical value** remains undetermined.

### Current Constant Hierarchy

The current structural order of appearance is:

- \(c\) — propagation structure.  
- \(\hbar\) — phase‑space structure.  
- \(\Lambda\) — spectral extent of projection.  
- \(G\) — curvature response.

### Next Target Constant: \(\alpha\)

The next target is the fine‑structure constant

\[
\alpha \approx \frac{1}{137},
\]

which measures the strength of electromagnetism.

In the spectral expansion of the Dirac operator:

- \(a_2\) term → curvature → gravity.  
- \(a_4\) term → gauge fields → electromagnetism.

Gravity appears earlier because it depends only on geometry; electromagnetism appears later because it depends on the structure of charged particles.

### Remaining Gap

To compute \(\alpha\), the framework must determine:

- **what particles exist**,  
- **what charges they carry**.

These properties fix the strength of electromagnetic interaction. The missing step is therefore:

\[
\text{projection} \;\rightarrow\; \text{particle spectrum and charges}.
\]

Once the projection uniquely determines particle charges, \(\alpha\) can in principle be derived from the same spectral framework.

## Consequences

- DDF provides a coherent mechanism for \(c, \hbar, G\), tying them to propagation, phase‑space, and spectral‑curvature structure of projection.  
- The remaining conceptual challenge is to show that projection determines a unique particle spectrum and charge assignment, enabling a derivation of \(\alpha\).  
- Progress on constants should be read together with F5 (structural role of constants) and the relevant operator notes (propagation rigidity, quantisation rigidity, spectral action).
